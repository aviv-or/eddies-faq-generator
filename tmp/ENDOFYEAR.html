END OF YEAR
By Lily

Project:

Ask people to describe and capture their 2013 year (hopes, challenges…)

Then we visualise how their year resonates with everyone else, this will be broken down by clusters.

We will pre-defined the categories (clusters) but we might add a few more based on the data.

We should be general in the end result and not specific to avoid moderation.
Questions should be straightforward and should not take long to complete. Personal questions should be asked at the end and kept separate.


So the challenge is:
How can we entice people to participate and share their 2013 year?
Because it’s obvious they know how was their year but what is the purpose for them to share their year with everyone else? What’s in there for them? Unless you see more personal data such as you VS your friends VS everyone...

We should be cleared on the following:

> What’s the outcome, purpose? what’s in it for me? What do I get from this?

> Be valuable: don’t waste my time

> How do we want user to feel like after using the interactive?

> Tell the user what we are going to do with their data?

> How do we engage people in? Social media strategy?Syndicated page?
> Can you access it via any device? responsive?
> How do we navigate to ‘Share your 2014’
> Be rewarding
> Let me share my results
> Be engaging, inviting. Tease people and show them a glance of You VS Everyone as you fill in the form. (eg: What’s your politics news.com.au)
> Don’t show me obvious data / questions. I want something interesting, compelling that i can share with my peers

PROCESS:

1. START SCREEN
2. 5 questions / category
3. Share a little about yourself (age, postcode, gender)
4. End result: You vs everyone

 We Feel Fine: An Almanac of Human Emotion —
At the core of We Feel Fine is a data collection engine that automatically scours the Internet every ten minutes, harvesting human feelings from a large number of blogs. Blog data comes from a variety of online sources. Once a sentence containing "I feel" or "I am feeling" is found, the system looks backward to the beginning of the sentence, and forward to the end of the sentence, and then saves the full sentence in a database.
A remarkable visual exploration of the 12 million human emotions collected since the project’s dawn. Infographic magic and data visualization wizardry make this massive repository of found sentiment incredibly personal yet incredibly relatable. From despair to exhilaration, from the public to the intimate, it captures the passions and dreams of which human existence is woven through candid vignettes, intelligent infographics and scientific observations.
